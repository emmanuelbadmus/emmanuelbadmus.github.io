About

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Electrical Engineering at the University of Vermont, working in the GOAL LAB under the supervision of Dr. Amritanshu Pandey. My research develops mathematical models, optimization algorithms, and AI-driven tools that make power grids smarter, cleaner, and more resilient.

Beyond the lab, I serve as Chief Technical Adviser at PAWATANK, guiding the design of solar-powered, NFC-enabled charging infrastructure for underserved communities in Africa.

My journey spans four institutions across three continents, giving me a global perspective on the energy challenges — and opportunities — facing the world today.

Core Expertise

Research Focus

  • Hosting Capacity Analysis: Assessing how much solar and DERs a feeder can absorb without compromising reliability or violating grid constraints.
  • Grid Simulation & Optimization: Developing algorithms for enhanced grid performance, stability, and economic dispatch in distribution networks.
  • Electrical Power Systems: In-depth study of power flow, system dynamics, control strategies, and protection coordination.
  • Multi-Renewable Integration: Architecting systems that seamlessly blend solar, wind, storage, and conventional generation at scale.
  • Circuit-Based Inverter Modeling: Creating detailed equivalent circuit models of inverters for steady-state and dynamic grid analysis.
  • Smart Energy Systems: Designing intelligent control and management systems for efficient energy distribution and demand response.

Tools & Platforms

  • Optimization & Analysis: Pyomo, Pandas, NumPy, SciPy, Matplotlib
  • Programming Languages: Python, Julia, MATLAB
